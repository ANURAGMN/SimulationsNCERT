✅ You are at (or very close to) θ = 90°. Here the triangle is right-angled at A and the area is maximum.

Geometry behind this: For fixed sides 6 and 10, the area is
Area = ½ · 6 · 10 · sin(θ) = 30·sin(θ). Since sin(θ) ≤ 1, the largest area occurs when sin(θ) = 1 → θ = 90°. At that point, the third side is x = √(6² + 10²) = 2√34.
